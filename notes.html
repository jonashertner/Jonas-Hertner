Quiet Currents of Change

2025-02-20 | Jonas Hertner

The world shifts quietly. A phrase appears, a new habit takes hold, something imperceptibly rearranges itself. Change is not an event but an atmosphere, a current that pulls without announcement.

The mind registers this as a subtle friction between expectation and reality. It does not arrive in epiphanies but in hesitation, in the pause before a response. The world moves faster than the mechanisms designed to process it.

The brain, as it always has, searches for patterns, for something solid to brace against. It senses motion without direction, a system altering according to rules hardly understood.

Some retreat into repetition, carving out rituals untouched by the churn. Others attempt to keep pace, consuming information at a rate beyond comprehension. Both are forms of seeking control, of resisting the sense that time is not simply moving but slipping.

If there is clarity to be found, it is in recognizing that emotions do not merely react to change; they mark it. The mind unsettles because it is learning, recalibrating, making room for what will soon feel as though it has always been there.
